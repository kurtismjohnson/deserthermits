The name "Desert Hermits" has been with me for a while in various ways, but here it points to how writing is deeply personal, but can still reach more broadly. Something solitary, carrying a message far and wide. Maybe others read the written words directly, or maybe writing for yourself, looking internally, makes you better for those around you. I think about the prayers of the monastics: a deeply personal endeavor that still serves a community, or perhaps even the entire world.

A photographer and writer, based in Atlanta, Georgia, who is interested in exploring the paradox of writing being both internal/personal and external/communal.

- Kurtis Johnson
